Section doree

You have a function f with his expression. You want to find a maximum or a minimum of the function. We will focus on the maximum. Considering the fact that you know briefly where a maximum of f is localised and the characteristics of f verifies : f increase and then decrease , in order that the maximum exist You take two real a and b as f(a) is not to far from f(b). This method use the golden number, alpha = ((sqrt(5)-1)/2. We define c=alpha*a+(1-alpha)*b and d=(1-alpha)*a+alpha*b . To approach the maximum of f between a and b, we will iterate number_of_iteration times this procedure:
-If f(a)<f(c) and f(c)>f(d) then b=d,c=alpha*a + (1-alpha)*d and d=c
-Else a=c , c=d and d=(1-alpha)*c + alpha*b

When the absolute value of a-b is less than our accuracy epsilon, we stop the loop.
Besides, we return the value of f((a+b)/2) and (a+b)/2. Finally, we can check if the algorithm returns a suitable value of the maximum of f between a and b.

Considering the function f:x->-x*x+4*x+19, we want to calcul the maximum of it. Let's say we calcul the zeros between a = -5 and b = 3, after 32 iterations of the procedure explained above, we obtain the maxmium value 2.09443. Moreover, the value of f(2.09443) = 22.9911. So, the result of the algorithm is quite satisfying.
