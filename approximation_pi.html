Approximation of PI

According to Newsweek, pi is useful for describing everything from the way light and sound waves ripple to the 'bendiness' of rivers, and it even crops up in calculations where circles are nowhere to be seen, such as Euler's Identity—a probability formula that has been described as the "most beautiful" in mathematics.

As pi is an irrational number, which means that it is a rela number that cannot expressed by a simple fraction. That's because pi is what mathematicians call an "infinite decimal" — after the decimal point, the digits go on forever and ever. Consequently, we have to calcul every number decimal after another until our precision satisfy us. Numerically, we tend to approximate the number pi by using different methods. The numerical methods which is famous is the Monte Carlo method.

Monte Carlo

The method is simple. Randomnly, we choose two real between -1 and 1. Let's called them x and y. Then, we check if the point of coordinate(x,y) is inside the circle of radius 1 and center 0. If the point of coordinate verifies the condition, therefore we add 1 to a variable called number_inside( which has been initialized at the beginning of the program). Then, we add 1 to a variable called number_of_iteration. We repeat exactly the procedure until of number_of_iteration reachs a integer maximum defined at the beginning of the program. And then we can calcul pi using number_of_iteration and number_inside

How do we calcul pi with number_of_iteration and number_inside ?

The answer use the figure above. The area of the green square is A_square = 2*2=4. The area of the circle inside the square is A_circle = pi*radius*radius=pi*1*1=pi. Now, let's understand what can we have done before. The quotient between number_inside and number_of_iteration should approach the quotient between A_circle and A_square. Consequently, pi=number_inside / number_of_iteration * 4 . As you may think, the precision of pi will be high when our number_of_iteration is a large integer.
With number_of_iteration = 100, we approximate pi = 3
With number_of_iteration = 1000, we approximate pi = 3.088
With number_of_iteration = 10000, we approximate pi = 3.1364
With number_of_iteration = 100000, we approximate pi = 3.1452
With number_of_iteration = 1000000, we approximate pi = 3.1404
With number_of_iteration = 10000000, we approximate pi = 3.14056
With number_of_iteration = 100000000, we approximate pi = 3.14177
